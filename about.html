How it all started


My journey into fitness did not as a sudden revelation, but as a gradual discovery of strength, resilience, and joy. Initially, it was about general well-being, but it quickly transformed into a deep passion that reshaped my daily life. I remember early on in my teenage years hows I laid on my bedroom floor watching petite girls doing pilates and copying them. Then I started with regular gym visits in the summer of 2022, drawn to the structured environment and the tangible progress in strength training.
The gym became my sanctuary, a place to push physical limits, build discipline, and witness my own progress. And along the ride I became fond of walking and exploring, slow movements that tranquils my body and mind. It became a powerful form of active meditation.
For me, fitness isn't just about physical health; it's a holistic practice that nurtures my mind and spirit. My journey is ongoing, always seeking new ways to challenge myself and explore the capabilities of the human body. I hope to continue exploring new trails, setting new personal bests, and inspiring others to find their own joy in movement.

Simple Meal Prep for the Week

  1. Choose a protein source (e.g., chicken, tofu) and cook in bulk.
  2. Roast a large pan of vegetables (e.g., broccoli, sweet potatoes) with seasoning.
  3. Cook a whole grain like white or brown rice.
  4. Portion out the protein, vegetables, and grain into individual containers.
  5. Store the containers in the fridge for easy, healthy meals throughout the week.

Weekly Workout Schedule

My typical training week to keep things balanced and effective.

Day Workout Focus Notes
Monday Legs (Squats, Deadlifts) Focus on heavy compound lifts.
Tuesday Cardio & Core 30 mins of running and core exercises.
Wednesday Rest or Active Recovery Light stretching or a leisurely walk.
Thursday Upper Body (Push) Bench press, overhead press, triceps.
Friday Upper Body (Pull) Pull-ups, rows, bicep curls.
Saturday Long Run or Hike Explore a new trail for 1-2 hours.
Sunday Rest Complete rest to allow for recovery.
